# 1.5 - Transmission Media and Transceivers

## Core Idea

Network media determine how bits travel. Transceivers and connectors determine how devices physically interface with those media and protocols.

## Wireless Media

### 802.11 Standards

- Define Wi-Fi behavior.
- Cover frequencies, channel widths, modulation, throughput, and compatibility.

### Cellular

- Uses carrier networks for wide-area wireless access.
- Common where mobility or backup WAN connectivity is needed.

### Satellite

- Supports connectivity where terrestrial links are limited.
- Often involves higher latency than typical wired broadband.

## Wired Media

### 802.3 Standards

- Define Ethernet over wired media.
- Cover physical signaling and Ethernet variants.

### Single-mode vs. Multimode Fiber

- Single-mode fiber supports very long distance using a narrow light path.
- Multimode fiber supports shorter distance and commonly lower-cost local fiber runs.
- Choice depends on distance, optics, and design goals.

### DAC Cable

- Direct attach copper uses integrated twinax-style cabling, often for short high-speed connections.

#### Twinaxial Cable

- A short-distance high-speed copper medium often used in DAC assemblies.

### Coaxial Cable

- Copper-based cable with strong shielding.
- Seen in some provider and legacy environments.

### Cable Speeds

- Different media types and standards support different throughput rates.
- Media choice affects speed, distance, and cost.

### Plenum vs. Non-plenum

- Plenum cable is rated for spaces used for air handling.
- Non-plenum cable is not interchangeable where building code requires plenum-rated cable.

## Transceivers

### By Protocol

#### Ethernet

- Optical or copper transceivers used for Ethernet links.

#### Fibre Channel

- Transceivers used for Fibre Channel storage networking.

### By Form Factor

#### SFP

- Small form-factor pluggable transceiver.
- Widely used for modular uplink or optical interfaces.

#### QSFP

- Quad small form-factor pluggable.
- Designed for higher-density or higher-bandwidth use cases.

## Connector Types

### SC

- Fiber connector with larger push-pull form.

### LC

- Small-form fiber connector common in modern installations.

### ST

- Older-style fiber connector using twist-lock behavior.

### MPO

- Multi-fiber connector for high-density fiber applications.

### RJ11

- Commonly associated with telephony cabling.

### RJ45

- Common Ethernet twisted-pair connector.

### F-type

- Commonly used with coaxial cable environments.

### BNC

- Bayonet-style coaxial connector seen in older and specialized applications.

## Media Selection Factors

- distance
- throughput
- electromagnetic environment
- connector compatibility
- cost
- building code
- protocol support

## Big Picture

A strong technician chooses media and transceivers based on link purpose, required distance, performance, and installation environment, not just on what connector happens to fit.
