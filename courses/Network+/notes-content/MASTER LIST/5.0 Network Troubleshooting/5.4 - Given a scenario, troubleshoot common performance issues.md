# 5.4 - Performance Issues

## Core Idea

Performance problems are not always total outages. They often appear as slowness, delay, instability, retransmissions, or poor user experience under load.

## Congestion / Contention

- Too many devices or flows compete for limited network resources.

## Bottlenecking

- One constrained component limits end-to-end performance.

## Bandwidth

- Available link capacity matters, but bandwidth alone does not equal good user experience.

### Throughput Capacity

- The amount of useful data that can actually pass through in practice.

## Latency

- Delay from source to destination.
- Affects real-time and interactive applications strongly.

## Packet Loss

- Missing packets force retransmissions or visible quality degradation.

## Jitter

- Variation in packet delay.
- Especially harmful to voice and video.

## Wireless Issues

### Interference

- Competing signals or noise reduce wireless quality.

#### Channel Overlap

- Overlapping channels create contention and interference problems.

### Signal Degradation or Loss

- Weak signal reduces reliability and throughput.

### Insufficient Wireless Coverage

- Coverage gaps create low signal, roaming, or complete connectivity issues.

### Client Disassociation Issues

- Clients drop off the network unexpectedly.

### Roaming Misconfiguration

- Poor roaming design creates sticky-client issues, handoff trouble, or poor mobility experience.

## Big Picture

Performance troubleshooting requires separating raw capacity problems from delay, loss, signal quality, and design inefficiency.
