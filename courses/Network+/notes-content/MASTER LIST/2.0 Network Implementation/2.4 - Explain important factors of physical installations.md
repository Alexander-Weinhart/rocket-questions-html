# 2.4 - Physical Installation Factors

## Core Idea

Physical installation choices affect safety, serviceability, airflow, power reliability, and the long-term health of network equipment.

## Installation Implications

### Locations

#### IDF

- Intermediate Distribution Frame supports local distribution closer to users or a building section.

#### MDF

- Main Distribution Frame is a central distribution location.

### Rack Size

- Equipment and accessories must fit the physical rack design.
- Rack planning affects expansion, cable management, and airflow.

### Port-side Exhaust / Intake

- Airflow direction matters in rack placement and thermal design.
- Wrong airflow alignment can overheat equipment.

### Cabling

#### Patch Panel

- Supports organized structured cabling terminations.

#### Fiber Distribution Panel

- Organizes and protects fiber connections.

### Lockable

- Physical security matters for networking spaces and equipment.

## Power

### UPS

- Uninterruptible Power Supply provides battery-backed temporary power.
- Helps bridge brief outages and support clean shutdowns.

### PDU

- Power Distribution Unit delivers power to multiple rack devices.

### Power Load

- You must understand total power draw so circuits and backup systems are not overloaded.

### Voltage

- Power requirements must match the equipment and site design.

## Environmental Factors

### Humidity

- Too much or too little humidity can damage hardware or increase static risk.

### Fire Suppression

- Network rooms need suppression approaches appropriate for equipment spaces.

### Temperature

- Excess heat reduces reliability and hardware lifespan.

## Big Picture

Network installation is not only about making equipment work on day one. It is about making it safe, maintainable, secure, and reliable over time.
