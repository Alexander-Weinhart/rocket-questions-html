# 4.2 - Attacks and Their Impact

## Core Idea

Attack knowledge helps you recognize how networks are abused, what symptoms those attacks create, and what controls are meant to reduce the damage.

## DoS / DDoS

- DoS overwhelms or disrupts a target from one source or vector.
- DDoS uses many sources at once.
- Impacts include service outage, congestion, and resource exhaustion.

## VLAN Hopping

- Attack aimed at reaching traffic in unauthorized VLANs.
- Often tied to poor trunking or VLAN design/security.

## MAC Flooding

- Attempts to overflow a switch MAC table.
- Can cause undesirable flooding behavior.

## ARP Poisoning / ARP Spoofing

- Corrupts IP-to-MAC trust on the local network.
- Can redirect traffic, enable interception, or disrupt connectivity.

## DNS Poisoning / DNS Spoofing

- Corrupts or falsifies DNS answers.
- Can redirect users to malicious destinations.

## Rogue Devices and Services

### Rogue DHCP

- Unauthorized DHCP can hand out wrong gateway, DNS, or addressing information.

### Rogue AP

- Unauthorized wireless access point can create a security gap or bypass policy.

## Evil Twin

- Fake wireless network designed to imitate a legitimate one and lure victims.

## On-path Attack

- Attacker positions themselves between communicating parties to observe or alter traffic.

## Social Engineering

- Uses human manipulation instead of purely technical exploitation.

### Phishing

- Fraudulent messages designed to trick users.

### Dumpster Diving

- Collecting sensitive information from discarded materials.

### Shoulder Surfing

- Observing sensitive information visually.

### Tailgating

- Gaining physical entry by following an authorized person.

## Malware

- Malicious software that can steal, disrupt, encrypt, spy, or provide unauthorized access.

## Why Impact Matters

- confidentiality can be broken
- integrity can be altered
- availability can be denied
- trust can be undermined
- recovery cost can become high

## Big Picture

Defenders need to understand not only attack names, but also what each attack targets, how it spreads, what symptoms it causes, and what type of control is appropriate against it.
