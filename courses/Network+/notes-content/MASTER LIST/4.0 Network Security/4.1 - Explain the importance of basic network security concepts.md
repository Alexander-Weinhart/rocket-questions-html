# 4.1 - Basic Network Security Concepts

## Core Idea

Network security protects confidentiality, integrity, availability, and controlled access across systems, identities, traffic, and physical environments.

## Logical Security

### Encryption

- Protects data from unauthorized reading.

#### Data in Transit

- Protects data while moving across the network.

#### Data at Rest

- Protects stored data on disks and other media.

### Certificates

- Used to establish identity and trust in many security systems.

#### PKI

- Public Key Infrastructure manages certificate trust relationships.

#### Self-signed

- Certificates signed by their own issuer rather than a trusted CA.
- Useful in limited scenarios but weaker for broad trust.

### IAM

- Identity and Access Management governs who can authenticate and what they can do.

#### Authentication

- Proves identity.

#### MFA

- Requires more than one factor for identity verification.

#### SSO

- Lets users access multiple systems after one authentication event.

#### RADIUS

- Centralized authentication protocol commonly used for network access.

#### LDAP

- Directory-access protocol often used in identity environments.

#### SAML

- Federation and authentication assertion technology used in web-based identity systems.

#### TACACS+

- Centralized authentication and authorization protocol often used for network-device administration.

#### Time-based Authentication

- Authentication methods that depend on time-based codes or validity.

#### Authorization

- Determines what an authenticated identity is permitted to do.

#### Least Privilege

- Grant only the access needed.

#### Role-based Access Control

- Permissions are assigned based on roles rather than only individual users.

### Geofencing

- Access control based on geographic criteria.

## Physical Security

### Camera

- Supports monitoring and deterrence.

### Locks

- Restrict physical access to equipment and spaces.

## Deception Technologies

### Honeypot

- Decoy system designed to attract or study attackers.

### Honeynet

- A broader deceptive environment of decoy systems.

## Common Security Terminology

### Risk

- The possibility of loss or harm.

### Vulnerability

- A weakness that can be exploited.

### Exploit

- The means used to take advantage of a vulnerability.

### Threat

- A potential source of harm.

### CIA Triad

- Confidentiality: prevent unauthorized disclosure.
- Integrity: prevent unauthorized alteration.
- Availability: keep systems and data accessible when needed.

## Audits and Regulatory Compliance

### Data Locality

- Where data is stored and processed matters legally and operationally.

### PCI DSS

- Security requirements related to payment-card environments.

### GDPR

- Regulatory framework for privacy and data protection.

## Network Segmentation Enforcement

### IoT and IIoT

- Connected operational and embedded devices often need separate security boundaries.

### SCADA / ICS / OT

- Industrial and operational systems often have unique availability and safety requirements.

### Guest

- Guest users should be separated from trusted internal resources.

### BYOD

- Personally owned devices introduce extra access-control and segmentation risk.

## Big Picture

Security is not one technology. It is layered control across identity, encryption, physical access, segmentation, and governance.
