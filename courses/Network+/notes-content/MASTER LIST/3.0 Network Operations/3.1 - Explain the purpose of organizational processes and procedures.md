# 3.1 - Organizational Processes and Procedures

## Core Idea

Reliable networks are not maintained only with technical skill. They also depend on documentation, life-cycle planning, change control, and consistent configuration management.

## Documentation

### Physical vs. Logical Diagrams

- Physical diagrams show actual hardware placement and cabling.
- Logical diagrams show how networks are segmented and connected conceptually.

### Rack Diagrams

- Show where equipment is mounted.
- Help with maintenance, planning, and power/cooling design.

### Cable Maps and Diagrams

- Show cable destinations, terminations, and pathways.

### Network Diagrams

#### Layer 1

- Focus on physical links and media.

#### Layer 2

- Focus on switching, VLANs, trunks, and local segmentation.

#### Layer 3

- Focus on IP networks, routing, and path design.

### Asset Inventory

#### Hardware

- Track what devices exist.

#### Software

- Track operating systems, tools, and installed components.

#### Licensing

- Track entitlement and compliance obligations.

#### Warranty Support

- Track support status and replacement eligibility.

### IPAM

- IP Address Management tracks address usage and assignment.

### SLA

- Service-Level Agreement defines service expectations and obligations.

### Wireless Survey / Heat Map

- Documents wireless coverage, expected signal behavior, and deployment planning.

## Life-cycle Management

### EOL

- End-of-life means the product is no longer being produced or sold in the normal way.

### EOS

- End-of-support means vendor support is ending or has ended.

### Software Management

#### Patches and Bug Fixes

- Keep systems stable and secure.

#### Operating System

- Version control and support status matter.

#### Firmware

- Device embedded software must also be managed carefully.

### Decommissioning

- Retire systems in a controlled and documented way.
- Include sanitization, removal from diagrams, and inventory updates.

## Change Management

### Request Process Tracking / Service Request

- Changes should be requested, reviewed, approved, tracked, and documented.
- Good change management reduces avoidable outages.

## Configuration Management

### Production Configuration

- The active configuration in use.

### Backup Configuration

- A recoverable saved copy.

### Baseline / Golden Configuration

- Known-good standard configuration for consistency and rebuilds.

## Big Picture

Operations maturity is what keeps networks supportable. Good documents, disciplined changes, and controlled configuration practices are as important as technical command knowledge.
