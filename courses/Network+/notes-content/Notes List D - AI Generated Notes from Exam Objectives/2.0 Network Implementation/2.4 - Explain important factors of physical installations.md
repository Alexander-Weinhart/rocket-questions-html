# 2.4 - Physical Installation Factors

> **Big idea:** Physical installation quality decides whether a network design survives contact with real rooms, racks, ceilings, power, heat, and cable runs. Good installation is part engineering, part documentation, and part not making future-you suffer.

## What you should be able to explain

- Explain rack, power, cooling, and cable-management concerns.
- Understand MDF/IDF roles and structured cabling.
- Recognize environmental risks and safety requirements.
- Connect physical choices to network reliability.

## Concept map

| Factor | Why it matters | Watch for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Power | Devices need stable power and sometimes PoE budgets | UPS sizing, redundant power, overloaded circuits |
| Cooling | Heat shortens equipment life and causes instability | Airflow, blocked vents, hot aisles |
| Cable management | Prevents damage and speeds troubleshooting | Labels, bend radius, strain relief |
| Rack layout | Supports airflow and serviceability | Weight, spacing, patch panels |
| Environment | Protects gear from damage | Moisture, dust, vibration, access control |

## MDF and IDF

- The MDF is the main distribution frame, often where provider links and core equipment land.
- IDFs serve floors or areas closer to users.
- Backbone cabling connects MDF and IDFs.
- Good labeling lets technicians trace a port without guessing.

## Power planning

- UPS systems keep equipment alive through short outages and power events.
- PoE budgets matter when switches power APs, phones, and cameras.
- Redundant power supplies only help if they connect to appropriately separate power sources.
- Generator and UPS planning should match business uptime needs.

## Cable care

- Copper and fiber both have bend-radius limits.
- Fiber connectors must stay clean.
- Cable trays, Velcro, and labels are boring until something breaks, then they are heroic.
- Avoid running data cabling near sources of electrical interference when possible.

## Scenario walkthrough

1. New access points randomly reboot after installation.
2. If they are PoE-powered, check the switch PoE budget and negotiated power class.
3. The wireless configuration may be fine while the physical power design is not.

## Exam cues

- Environmental answers are often correct when failures are intermittent or location-specific.
- PoE budget is about total available switch power, not just port count.
- Documentation and labeling are part of physical installation quality.
