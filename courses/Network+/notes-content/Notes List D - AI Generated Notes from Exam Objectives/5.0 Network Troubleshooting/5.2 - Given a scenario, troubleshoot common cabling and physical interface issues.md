# 5.2 - Cabling and Physical Interface Issues

> **Big idea:** Physical problems are often simple, but they can create confusing symptoms. Bad cables, wrong transceivers, dirty fiber, duplex mismatches, low signal, damaged ports, and PoE problems can all look like higher-layer failures until the physical layer is checked.

## What you should be able to explain

- Recognize cable, connector, transceiver, and interface symptoms.
- Explain link lights, errors, speed/duplex, and PoE clues.
- Choose appropriate physical troubleshooting tools.
- Understand wireless physical-layer issues such as interference and signal loss.

## Concept map

| Symptom | Likely physical clue | Good check |
| --- | --- | --- |
| No link | Cable, port, optic, speed mismatch | Link lights, swap cable, show interface |
| Errors/CRC | Bad cable, interference, duplex issue | Interface counters, cable tester |
| PoE device reboots | Power budget or cable issue | PoE status and budget |
| Fiber link down | Wrong optic, dirty connector, polarity | Optic type, light levels, clean connectors |
| Wireless drops | Interference or weak signal | Wi-Fi analyzer, channel plan |

## Copper issues

- Open, short, split pair, and incorrect pinout can break or degrade links.
- Cable category and run length affect supported speeds.
- EMI can corrupt signaling when cabling is routed poorly.
- A cable tester can reveal problems that are invisible from the desk.

## Fiber issues

- Dirty fiber connectors are a common cause of loss.
- Transmit and receive strands must be oriented correctly.
- Single-mode and multimode optics/cables must match the design.
- Light levels can show whether the signal is too weak or too strong.

## Interface counters

- CRC errors point toward frame corruption.
- Input/output errors and discards can reveal congestion or physical trouble.
- Speed and duplex mismatches can cause poor performance even when the link is up.

## Scenario walkthrough

1. A workstation connects at 100 Mbps even though the switch and NIC support 1 Gbps.
2. Check cable category, damaged pairs, patch panel path, and autonegotiation.
3. A single bad pair can force a lower speed or unstable link.

## Exam cues

- Start with Layer 1 when the link itself is questionable.
- CRC errors are a strong physical/data-link clue.
- Fiber problems often come down to cleanliness, optic mismatch, or polarity.
